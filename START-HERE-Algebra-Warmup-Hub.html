§ 01

The Diagnostic

Run this in the first week of school. Fifteen items, thirty minutes. Results stream live into the data sheet.

Algebra Prerequisite Diagnostic · 15 items · 30 minutes

Day-1 baseline for the cohort.

What it measures: 8th grade core (integers, equations, slope, Pythagorean Theorem) plus four Algebra: C&C lookahead items (radicals, function notation, distance formula). All four-option selected response.

What's included: Built-in Desmos calculator, on-screen formula sheet, flag-for-review, 30-minute timer with auto-submit, and a review screen before submission.

Logging: Each submission writes to two Google Sheets tabs — a one-row-per-student Submissions summary and a long-format Item Detail tab for domain analysis.

§ 02

The Daily Routine

Ten minutes. Same shape every week. Students stop relearning the procedure and start working.

Monday
Introduce
DOK 1 · 8 min

Worked example by teacher. One parallel item for students. Lowest demand of the week.

Tuesday
Practice
DOK 1-2 · 10 min

2-3 items with light variation. Individual first, then 60-second partner check.

Wednesday
Extend
DOK 2 · 10 min

One item pushing the skill into a new context. Whole-class debrief on at least one path.

Thursday
Apply / Spiral
DOK 2-3 · 10 min

One application item plus one spiraled item from a previous week. Keeps prior learning warm.

Friday
Check
DOK 1-3 · 10 min

5-item quick check. Low stakes, collected. Signal on whether to advance or reteach Monday.

§ 06

Checklists

The two moments that matter most for this routine to land: launch week and the three checkpoint Fridays.

Launch Week (Week of Aug 4)

Before students arrive · then Day 1, 2, 3
  • Test the diagnostic on your own device. Submit a fake student to confirm the data sheet writes correctly.
  • Print or post the formula sheet on the wall as a visible classroom anchor.
  • Day 1: Norms. Tell students what the warmup is and is not. Specifically: it is not graded, it is for fluent recall.
  • Day 2: Administer the diagnostic. Students bring a device or use the lab.
  • Day 3: Open the data sheet. Sort by L1/L2/L3 to confirm tier groupings against your read of the room.
  • Day 4-5: Begin the regular Mon-Fri warmup routine using the W1-2 deck.

Checkpoint Fridays (W6, W12, W18)

The three data points that drive instructional decisions
  • Week 6 (Sept 11): Administer the 12-item Phase 1 checkpoint. Compare scores to the Day-2 diagnostic. Re-tier students.
  • Week 12 (Oct 23): Administer the 15-item Phase 2 checkpoint. Share data with the math department.
  • Week 18 (Dec 11): Administer the 20-item Semester Close Assessment. Generate the semester data summary.
  • Loop back to MCMS: send Week 12 and Week 18 data to the sending 8th grade teachers. Close the data loop.
  • Re-deploy the data sheet view each checkpoint so the receiving teacher always sees the most recent picture first.
